Art manifesto

These are my values I stick to in my creative works. In a no way, shape, or form it's a dogma that everyone must adhere to, but I rely on them when producing new ideas and considering a collaboration. It's just for my own self. If you like this manifesto, you are free to use it and link it on your website!


  1. Creation is a result of conscious observation. A great artist is a great viewer. A great viewer has the ability to analyze, intertwine, and expand upon what they see.

    They not only see, but utilize other senses to understand the subject comprehensively. For me it's a lot about tactility: the texture of paint, paper, layers; the feeling of stroking a line with a brush, and you sense it as if you are a brush itself. You feel how paint interacts with paper and you know it well, which facilitates control over the interaction. If it's a digital drawing, it's the feeling of the cold surface you draw on.

    Artists don't just see a still image, but interactions and relationships between objects, how they form a system that makes a composition.

  2. Art is an exploration of life, sensations, and relationships. It studies, makes experiments, and helps us understand and live the life to the fullest.

  3. All forms of art are equal, no kind of art is right or wrong. The artist is not limited to a certain, 'more valid' goal, theme, medium, or style â€” there's no such thing. Art can be uncanny and whimsical, or mundane and hyperrealistic. It's free to express all facets of consciousness and life.

  4. All art is art, no matter the purpose or lack of thereof. Do not define art by moral value or 'usefulness'.

    If we are to define art by its 'benevolence', we need a solid definition of 'benevolence', but it's subjective and ever-changing.

    If we are to define art by its 'usefullness' or 'meaningfullness', we fall pray to the exploitative nature of capitalism where everything must be used in some way. It's degrading.

    I refuse to define art to prevent constructing a hierarchy. We are not 'better people' because we make art, 'good' and 'real' art at that.

  5. No one must ever be shunned and barred to entry the art sphere. Disability, poverty, or lack of skill are aspects of sentience and thus of art.

  6. Art is made possible by sentience (traits of which include observational and analytical skills), thus no machine is able to recreate art.

  7. The greatest value of art is the process of creating it. The result encapsulates an established relationship between the subject and the artist, ups and downs of this relationship, and efforts poured into it.

  8. Art must be redefined not as a commodity or worse â€” a luxury, â€” but as a common good. It is best to share art freely for everyone to experience, whenever possible.

  9. Art is inseparable from the artist for the artist projects their views, feelings, and experiences onto their craft in explicit or â€” more often â€” implicit ways.

To sum up: observe, explore, reflect, share, and don't judge.

Ways to collaborate

  • Trading. I like when people draw me in the way they personally see me, letting their imagination loose. If you think it'd fit for me to be a purple dragon, draw that purple dragon! And I would reciprocate with anything you'd like me to draw that isn't in my 'dont's'.
  • 'Draw This In Your Style' challenge. And I mean it when I say 'challenge'!
  • Cover art for music albums. I'm a metalhead, and I particularly enjoy post-metal, atmospheric black, and folk, but also some post-rock too. If you're a small band or artist beginning their journey, we could work together.
