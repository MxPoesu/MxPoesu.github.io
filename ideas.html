Art manifesto

These are my values I stick to in my creative works. In a no way, shape, or form it's a dogma that everyone must adhere to, but I rely on them when producing new ideas and considering a collaboration. It's just for my own self. If you like this manifesto, you are free to use it and link it on your website!


  1. Creation is a result of painstaking observation. A great artist is a great viewer.

  2. Art is an exploration of life, sensations, and relationships. It is like a natural science on its own â€” it studies, makes experiments, and helps us understand and live the life to the fullest.

  3. All forms of art are equal, no kind of art is right or wrong. The artist is not limited to a certain, 'more valid' goal, theme, medium, style, etc â€” there's no such thing. Art can amaze, mock, cheer up, sadden, intrigue, scare, arouse, disgust, inspire, disappoint, support, call to question, be a fairytale, be dystopian, and much more. It can be uncanny and whimsical, or mundane and hyperrealistic. It's free to express all multifacetedness of consciousness.

  4. All art is art, as long as it's made by sentient beings, no matter the purpose. Do not define art by moral value or 'usefulness'. Art too can be ulterior, and we need to remember that to not fall prey to the notion that we are automatically 'better' people because we make 'good' and 'real' art. Our perception could be distorted as non of us are infallible and immune to propaganda, and we, as artists, need to constantly reflect on our core values to prevent any harm.

  5. No one must ever be shunned and barred to entry the art sphere. Disability, poverty, or lack of skill are aspects of sentience and thus of art.

  6. As art expresses sentience, no machine is able to recreate art.

  7. The greatest value of art is the process of creating it.

  8. Art must be redefined not as a commodity or worse â€” a luxury, â€” but as a common good. It is best to share art freely for everyone to experience, whenever possible.

  9. Art is inseparable from the artist for the artist projects their views, feelings, and experiences onto their craft in explicit or â€” more often â€” implicit ways.

To sum up: observe, explore, reflect, share, and don't judge.

Ways to collaborate

  • Trading, if I like your style. I like when people draw me in the way they personally see me, letting their imagination loose. If you think it'd fit for me to be a purple dragon, draw that purple dragon! And I would reciprocate with anything you'd like me to draw that isn't in my 'dont's'.
  • 'Draw This In Your Style' challenge. And I mean it when I say 'challenge'! If you think I'd be inspired by your work and it'd look curious in my artstyle, go ahead and hit me up.
  • Cover art for music albums. I'm a metalhead, and I particularly enjoy post-metal, atmospheric black, and folk, but also some post-rock too. If you're a small band or artist beginning their journey and I like your music, we could work together, for starters even for free.
